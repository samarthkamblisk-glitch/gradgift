The problem

A broken graduation tradition

Every year, 4 million students graduate and enter the workforce. They're moving to expensive cities, starting demanding jobs, and need thousands of dollars in career essentials — all at once.

What do they get? Hallmark cards, $50 Venmo, and generic Amazon gift cards. Cash feels impersonal. Asking feels awkward. No one wins.

"I'm moving to NYC for consulting. I need a whole new wardrobe. I'm not sending my family a GoFundMe for clothes."

— ILR Senior, Cornell '26

"My aunt keeps asking what I want. I don't want cash, but I also don't want a random sweater from Macy's."

— A&S Senior, Cornell '26

We spoke to 15 Cornell seniors. 100% needed gear. 0% felt comfortable asking.

Our solution

Professional investment, not a handout

GradGift reframes the graduation gift as a professional investment in a young person's career — not a request for charity.

Gifters know what they're buying into. Graduates get what they need. The stigma disappears.
